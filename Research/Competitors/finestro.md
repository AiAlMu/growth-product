# Finestro

**URL:** https://finestro.io
**Category:** AI Tools Mastery / Freelancing Education / Mobile Learning App
**One-liner:** A practical AI tools learning app aimed at helping beginners confidently use AI for work and freelancing, with personalized onboarding and step-by-step learning paths.

---

## Business Model

Subscription-based with a paid trial. Quiz-based onboarding personalizes the user's learning path. Free version available with limited access; subscription unlocks all lessons and modules. Uses a short introductory trial period to hook users, followed by auto-renewal at full price. Significant complaints about inability to cancel and unauthorized recurring charges.

## Target Audience

- People seeking alternative income sources
- Those dissatisfied with traditional employment
- Individuals concerned about financial security
- Beginners with no AI background wanting hands-on guidance
- Freelancers wanting to build confidence with AI tools
- The onboarding quiz segments users into 4 profiles based on their income/career challenges

## Product & Features

- **Quiz-based onboarding**: Personalizes learning path based on user challenges and goals
- **Step-by-step AI learning paths**: Structured curriculum covering real-world AI use cases
- **Freelancing skills focus**: Lessons aimed at building confidence to start a freelance business
- **Mobile apps**: iOS and Android
- **AI tools coverage**: Work, study, and everyday AI applications
- **Free tier**: Limited access to learning materials
- **Paid tier**: Full access to all lessons and modules
- Marketed via: "Start freelancing and grow your income with AI"
- Limited details publicly available on curriculum depth or specific tools covered

## vs. JobEscape: Where They Win

- Quiz-based personalization at onboarding is a smoother UX approach
- Free tier lowers the barrier to entry compared to paid-only platforms
- Mobile apps on both iOS and Android
- "Start freelancing and grow your income" messaging aligns exactly with target audience
- Simple, beginner-focused positioning reduces overwhelm for first-time AI learners

## vs. JobEscape: Where JobEscape Wins

- JobEscape teaches real automation technology (N8N, Make.com) — Finestro's curriculum appears surface-level
- JobEscape has been in the market longer and has more established trust signals
- Finestro's 2.5/5 App Store rating and 8 reviews is a severe credibility disadvantage
- JobEscape has clearer company identity; Finestro has almost zero transparency
- Finestro's billing practices are widely condemned; users actively warning others away
- JobEscape's WhatsApp automation and AI agent features are more technically differentiated
- Trust score of 35/100 vs. JobEscape's legitimate market presence

## Key Weaknesses / User Complaints

- **Cannot cancel**: Multiple users report the company makes it "nearly impossible to cancel" and continues charging cards after cancellation requests
- **Unauthorized charges**: Customers report continued billing weeks or months after requesting cancellation
- **No refunds**: Users report automated replies to refund requests with no actual resolution
- **"DO NOT USE/BUY!!"**: Direct App Store reviews call it a scam, citing inability to get money back
- **Very low App Store rating**: 2.5/5 from only 8 reviews is an extremely poor signal
- **New domain + low trust score**: Created March 2025, trust score 35/100 — raises legitimacy questions
- **Opaque company**: No named team, no company background, no Crunchbase, no LinkedIn
- **Content depth unknown**: Insufficient public information to assess curriculum quality

## Notes

- Finestro appears to be a new market entrant (March 2025) operating a Coursiv/Tixu-style playbook
- The "com.applyft" Android developer ID suggests it may be built by an app development studio that builds multiple subscription apps using the same template
