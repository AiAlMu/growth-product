# Make Academy

**URL:** https://academy.make.com
**Category:** No-Code Automation Learning / Official Platform Education
**One-liner:** Make.com's free, self-paced official learning platform teaching users to build automations and AI agents using Make — with 200,000+ learners and a direct path to Make Certification.

---

## Business Model

Free learning platform (no subscription). Make Academy is a free educational offering from Make.com (formerly Integromat), one of the leading no-code automation platforms. The Academy serves as a customer acquisition and retention tool for Make.com's paid subscription platform. Users learn for free, then build automations on Make's paid plans. Certifications available upon completion.

## Pricing

| Plan | Price |
|------|-------|
| Make Academy (all courses) | Free |
| Make.com Core plan | ~$9–$16/month (for running automations, 300K credits/month) |
| Make.com Pro plan | ~$29+/month (8M credits/month) |
| Teams/Enterprise | Custom |

- The Academy itself is completely free
- Make.com platform plans are required to actually run automations in production
- Switching to credit-based billing in August 2025
- Professional support, Academy training, and community included on all paid Make plans at no extra cost

## Target Audience

- Non-technical professionals wanting to automate workflows

## Product & Features

- **36 interactive courses** covering fundamentals to advanced agentic AI automation

- **Hands-on automation-as-you-learn** format — build real automations during lessons

- **Self-paced**: Learn on your schedule, no cohort timing
- **6-month access** per course enrollment (badges retained permanently)
- **Official curriculum**: Created by Make.com team — most authoritative Make training available
- **Community access**: Make Community included with Make.com plans
- Total certification: ~15 hours for full Foundation path
- 200,000+ learners have completed courses

## vs. JobEscape: Where They Win

- Make Academy teaches the exact tool (Make.com) that JobEscape's own curriculum is built on — users learning automation for freelancing will encounter both
- Completely free — zero cost barrier to entry
- Official curriculum from the tool creator = authoritative and always up-to-date
- 200,000+ learners and Celonis/Make brand authority dwarfs all others in this competitive set
- Full certification pathway with recognized credentials in the automation space
- Best-in-class depth for Make.com specifically
- Strong job market demand: "Automation specialist" is one of Upwork's fastest-growing freelance categories

## vs. JobEscape: Where JobEscape Wins

- JobEscape is the full package: tools + training + freelancing pathway + community + mobile app
- Make Academy teaches the tool but not how to find clients, price services, or run a freelance business
- JobEscape bundles Make.com access within the subscription (no need for separate paid Make plan)
- JobEscape is mobile-first; Make Academy is web-only
- JobEscape teaches N8N *and* Make, giving platform flexibility; Make Academy is single-platform
- Make Academy has no freelancing income framework — it's tool training, not career training
- JobEscape's AI agents, ChatGPT/Claude/Gemini access, and WhatsApp automation are out of scope for Make Academy
- JobEscape's subscription (~$30/month) is all-in; Make Academy free + Make.com paid plan can cost similar or more for active users

## Key Weaknesses / User Complaints

- **Not a career platform**: Teaches Make.com usage only — no income path, no client acquisition, no freelance framework
- **Web-only**: No mobile app; significant friction for learners who prefer mobile
- **Steep learning curve**: Make's own team recommends ~19 hours of training before building production workflows — long for casual learners
- **Tied to Make.com ecosystem**: Learning Make doesn't help if clients use Zapier or N8N
- **No community of freelancers**: Community is tool-users, not income-seekers
- **Requires separate paid plan**: Free learning → still need to pay for Make to run automations
- **Complex UX for beginners**: Make's interface, while no-code, is described as "tricky" for absolute beginners

## Notes

- Make Academy is a **strategic reference** for JobEscape, not a head-to-head competitor — it validates that Make.com skills are in-demand and JobEscape's curriculum is on-target
- If a user discovers Make Academy before JobEscape, they get the tool training but not the income path — JobEscape can position as "the income layer on top of Make"
- The transition to credit-based billing in 2025 may confuse users and increase appetite for a bundled platform like JobEscape
- Make Academy's 200K+ learners represent a potential acquisition audience for JobEscape (they've learned the tool, now they need the freelancing framework)
- Partnership/integration opportunity: JobEscape could position itself as the "how to make money with Make.com" companion to Make Academy
