# Zeely

**URL:** https://zeely.ai
**Category:** AI Ad Creative Platform / AI Marketing Automation
**One-liner:** An AI-powered advertising platform for e-commerce sellers and small businesses to create UGC-style video ads, static ad creatives, and run multi-platform campaigns — no design skills needed.

---

## Business Model

Subscription-based SaaS with tiered feature access and credit-based usage for ad creation. Also generates revenue by charging a percentage (~12%) on ad spend managed through the platform on top of monthly subscription fees. Free tier includes 1,000 credits and basic templates. First payment is heavily discounted to drive conversions.

## Pricing

| Starter | $29.95/month (reg. $49.95) | 500 | Most accessible |

- All three plans include identical features; only credit allocation differs
- Plans available in monthly, bi-monthly, quarterly, and weekly durations
- First-period heavy discount; renews at regular price
- Additional cost: running actual ads on Facebook/Instagram involves separate ad spend, with Zeely charging ~12% on ad spend managed through the platform
- Upgrade/downgrade available anytime in-app
- Cancel anytime without contacting support

## vs. JobEscape: Where They Win

- Not directly comparable — Zeely is an AI ad tool, not an education platform
- Strong funding ($1M), investor validation, and a real company with named founders
- Exceptional App Store presence: 4.6/5 with 18,000 ratings — one of the strongest in this competitive set
- Clear, tangible ROI for users (ad performance improvement)
- Already proven at scale: 650K+ businesses
- Legitimate product with genuine use case (ad creation) vs. education-only platforms
- Cancel-anytime policy is cleaner than most competitors

## vs. JobEscape: Where JobEscape Wins

- Completely different product category: Zeely is an ad tool, not a freelance education platform
- JobEscape teaches skills that create income; Zeely serves existing business owners who already have income to spend on ads
- Zeely requires ad budget ($500–$1,000+/month) to get ROI; JobEscape targets people with no existing business
- JobEscape appeals to career-changers and income-seekers; Zeely targets existing marketers/sellers
- Zeely's 12% fee on ad spend is an additional cost on top of already-expensive subscription
- JobEscape's automation curriculum (N8N, Make.com) teaches durable technical skills vs. Zeely's tool-specific dependency

## Key Weaknesses / User Complaints

- **Unauthorized charges**: Multiple App Store reviews report being charged $119+ unexpectedly, with cancellation giving "the runaround"
- **Hidden costs**: The 12% ad spend fee is not prominently disclosed during initial signup
- **Total cost opacity**: Subscription + ad spend + platform fee can add up to significantly more than advertised
- **Billing complaints on Trustpilot**: Recurring theme of charges after trial cancellation
- **Not for zero-ad-budget users**: Requires existing ad spend to generate value; unsuitable for true beginners
- **Niche use case**: Only useful if you're actively running paid social ads — irrelevant for organic-focused businesses

## Notes

- Zeely is a legitimate, VC-backed company with real product — it's in a different category than Coursiv/Tixu clones
- The strongest direct overlap with JobEscape is in serving the "non-technical person who wants to make money with AI" segment — but via marketing automation rather than freelancing education
- Zeely is the most credible company in this competitive set by funding, team, and scale
- The ad creation angle could be taught as a freelance service within a platform like JobEscape (e.g., "become an AI ads freelancer")
- Watch for Zeely potentially expanding into education/training features as a competitive move
