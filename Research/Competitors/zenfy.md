# Zenfy

**URL:** https://zenfy.ac
**Category:** AI Tools Learning App / Job Discovery / Professional Growth Platform
**One-liner:** A hybrid professional growth platform combining AI tools learning, curated career resources, and a remote job board — positioned as "your gateway to mastery and professional growth."

---

## Business Model

Subscription-based with a low-cost trial period. Operates as a personal growth dashboard — part e-learning hub, part job board, part content curation engine. Trial pricing used to acquire users, with recurring billing afterward. Has significant complaints about opaque billing and difficulty canceling.

## Pricing

**Confirmed at checkout:**

| Plan | Intro price (with promo) | Full price (auto-renews at) |
|---|---|---|
| **1-week plan** | **$10.00** (promo code "Discount_Apr50") | **$20.00/week** |

- Weekly recurring billing — renews every 7 days
- Full price $20/week = **~$87/month** annualized — highest in the competitive set by far
- 50% promo codes distributed widely (likely in ads); promo code name visible: "Discount_Apr50"
- Users may not realize they're being billed $20/week after the intro period
- No multi-week or monthly plan shown — single weekly tier only (confirmed)
- Pricing not displayed on the website; only revealed at checkout after quiz
- Subscriptions auto-renew; cancellation process described as "clunky"
- Only 50% refunds offered per user reports
- Additional unexplained charges also reported by users

## Target Audience

- People looking for remote jobs and career transitions
- Beginners wanting to learn AI tools for career growth
- Career-changers seeking structured learning paths
- Job seekers in global markets (US, Canada, Australia appear in Trustpilot reviews)
- Users drawn by "professional growth" and "find high-paying remote jobs" marketing

## Product & Features

- **AI tools learning app**: Step-by-step lessons on using AI tools
- **Remote job board**: Curated remote job listings with matching based on user skills
- **Personalized feed**: News updates, articles, and career resources
- **Learning paths**: Structured progression for professional development
- **Mobile app**: Available on Google Play (Android)
- **Onboarding quiz**: Personalizes recommendations and learning path
- Content described by reviewers as "mainly a personalized feed with links and articles" rather than deep structured courses
- Not purely a course platform — hybrid between news aggregator, job board, and learning app

## vs. JobEscape: Where They Win

- Hybrid approach (learning + job board) is theoretically more complete than pure education
- Remote job board feature directly addresses "how do I find clients?" question
- $2M+ monthly traffic suggests meaningful market traction
- Content feed approach requires less production overhead than full courses
- "Professional growth dashboard" positioning is broader and potentially more appealing to job seekers
- Trial pricing is accessible

## vs. JobEscape: Where JobEscape Wins

- JobEscape teaches real technical skills (N8N, Make.com automation) vs. Zenfy's article/link curation
- JobEscape's automation bot curriculum creates genuinely marketable skills
- Zenfy's content quality is low — described as "links and articles" not actual courses
- Zenfy's job board legitimacy is questioned by users ("job opportunities on the platform" are uncertain)
- JobEscape has clearer company identity; Zenfy has very low transparency
- Zenfy's billing practices are a serious trust liability
- Zenfy's mobile presence is tiny (5K downloads); JobEscape is mobile-first

## Key Weaknesses / User Complaints

- **Billing confusion**: Users report being charged $40 when they expected $5 trial, plus unexplained additional charges
- **Cancellation difficulty**: Multiple reviews describe the cancellation process as clunky, with continued charges after requesting cancellation
- **Only 50% refunds**: Refund policy offers half-refunds only, not full refunds — highly unusual and widely criticized
- **Content quality**: Described as a "personalized feed with links and articles" rather than structured courses — users feel misled about the depth of the platform
- **Job board legitimacy**: Some users question whether the job opportunities are real or curated well
- **Tiny team**: Support appears to be run by one person, leading to slow responses
- **Low downloads**: 5K+ Google Play downloads is very small for a paid subscription platform
- **ScamAdviser flags**: Mixed trust signals due to unusual domain (.ac) and opaque company background

## Notes

- Zenfy appears to be a "value proposition ambiguity" product: markets itself as a career growth platform but delivers a content feed with limited course depth
- The .ac domain (Ascension Island) is unconventional for a consumer app — possibly chosen for novelty or availability
- Traffic of 2M/month despite poor app metrics suggests web-based funnel (landing page → sign-up → subscription) rather than organic app discovery
- The hybrid job board + learning approach is a reasonable angle but execution appears shallow
- Direct comparison class: more similar to a content/career platform than a true AI education app like Coursiv or Tixu
