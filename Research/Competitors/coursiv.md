# Coursiv

**URL:** https://coursiv.io / https://home.coursiv.io / https://coursiv.com
**Category:** AI Upskilling / Mobile-First Education / Freelance Career Training
**One-liner:** Mobile-first AI skills platform with 15-minute daily lessons and 28-day challenges, targeting busy professionals who want to use AI for side income and career growth.

---

## Business Model

Subscription-based (recurring monthly or annual). No per-course purchases. One plan unlocks everything. 3-day free trial available on iOS and Android only (not web). Significant promotional pricing used in ads to drive trial sign-ups, followed by full-price auto-renewal — a key source of complaints.

- Annual plan also available at ~$16–$25/month billed annually

## Target Audience

- Professionals who are not programmers
- Beginners intimidated by AI
- Freelancers seeking to add AI skills to their offering
- Small business owners exploring AI for marketing, content, automation
- Career-changers looking for income via AI side hustles
- Core demographic: US, India, Canada

## Product & Features

- **28-day challenge format**: Daily progressive lessons (~15 minutes each)
- **Four core challenge tracks**: AI Mastery, Social Media, Dropshipping, Remote Work
- **Tools covered**: ChatGPT, Midjourney, DALL-E, Jasper AI, Stable Diffusion, Claude, Copilot, Google Gemini
- **Built-in playground**: Integrated ChatGPT-4 and DALL-E access for practice (no separate subscription needed)
- **Dual-format lessons**: Read or listen
- **Gamification**: Streaks, progress tracking, daily challenges
- **Certificates**: Completion certificates shareable on LinkedIn
- **Mobile apps**: iOS and Android
- **Web access**: Available at app.coursiv.io
- Does NOT provide job placement, client connections, or accredited credentials
- Content is beginner-level; not designed for developers or ML practitioners

## Company Background

- **HQ**: Limassol, Cyprus

- Operates separately from coursiv.com (which appears to be a related or rebranded domain)
- Heavy paid advertising across Meta and Google to drive top-of-funnel

## vs. JobEscape: Where They Win

- Massive scale: 800K+ paid users, 1M+ app downloads vs. JobEscape's much smaller base
- Significantly more brand recognition and social proof (122K+ reviews)
- Higher App Store ratings and volume
- More polished app experience with gamification
- Broader tool coverage (ChatGPT, Midjourney, DALL-E, etc.)
- Stronger web presence (~11M monthly visits)
- Challenge format is engaging and retention-friendly
- Built-in AI tool access within the platform (no separate subscriptions needed)

## vs. JobEscape: Where JobEscape Wins

- JobEscape has a clearer focus on actual automation and bot-building (N8N, Make.com) vs. Coursiv's softer "use AI tools" curriculum
- JobEscape explicitly teaches freelance client acquisition and monetization workflows
- Coursiv content is widely criticized as too basic — largely available free on YouTube
- JobEscape's automation focus is more directly tied to billable freelance skills
- No job placement or income-path infrastructure at Coursiv
- JobEscape's AI agents / WhatsApp automation are more technically sophisticated

## Key Weaknesses / User Complaints

- **Subscription trap**: Low intro price buries auto-renewal at full rate ($39.99/month); cancellation is confusing (must be done through Google/Apple, no in-app button)
- **Content is too basic**: Multiple Reddit and review threads call lessons "surface-level" and say the content is freely available on YouTube
- **Refund disputes**: Users report unauthorized charges after thinking they canceled; support is slow to respond
- **No job outcomes**: No client connections, job board, or income path — just certificates
- **Heavy advertising vs. actual value gap**: Marketing promises $2K+/month; curriculum doesn't directly support that claim
- Facebook page tagged "BEWARE of Coursiv course TRAP" in multiple posts

## Notes

- Coursiv is the clear market leader in this category by volume; it essentially defines the template that Tixu, Finestro, Zenfy, and others are copying
- The brand has two main domains (coursiv.io and coursiv.com) which may indicate a rebrand or separate regional entity
- Trustpilot shows 3,200+ five-star reviews but also significant negative patterns around billing
- Despite complaints, the sheer volume of users means it generates substantial revenue; bootstrapped profitability is plausible
