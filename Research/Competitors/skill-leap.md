# Skill Leap (by Futurepedia)

**URL:** https://skillleap.futurepedia.io / https://futurepedia.io/ai-education-platform
**Category:** AI Upskilling / Subscription Learning Platform / Entrepreneurs & Professionals
**One-liner:** A curated AI education membership for entrepreneurs and professionals, offering 30+ expert courses, 1,000+ videos, 5,500+ prompts, and weekly content updates on the latest AI tools.

---

## Business Model

Subscription-based membership with all-inclusive access — no per-course fees. Web-based platform accessible via browser on any device (mobile, tablet, desktop). Uses a free trial to convert to paid membership. Grandfathering strategy: early subscribers lock in pricing as rates increase over time, creating urgency to join. Affiliated with Futurepedia, a well-known AI tools directory.

## Pricing

| Plan | Price |
|------|-------|
| Monthly All Access | $29.99/month (down from $39.99) |
| Yearly All Access | ~$20/month billed annually (~$240/year) (down from $39.99/month) |
| Free Trial | 7 days (no charge) |

- All plans include full access to all courses, tools, prompts, and community
- Price lock-in offered to early subscribers ("grandfathered" rate)
- No per-course charges; no tiers or upsells
- Clean, transparent pricing model — a strong trust differentiator vs. competitors

## vs. JobEscape: Where They Win

- Clean, transparent pricing with no subscription traps or hidden fees
- Free 7-day trial is no-risk for users
- Backed by Futurepedia's authority in the AI tools space — strong credibility
- 5,500+ prompt templates is a genuinely useful resource no competitor matches at this scale
- Weekly content updates ensure curriculum stays current with rapidly evolving AI tools
- Grandfathering strategy creates loyalty and reduces churn
- Broad entrepreneurial focus attracts users beyond just "freelancing" niche
- No billing complaints in reviews — clean reputation

## vs. JobEscape: Where JobEscape Wins

- JobEscape teaches real automation building (N8N, Make.com bots) vs. Skill Leap's tool-usage orientation
- JobEscape is mobile-first with a native app; Skill Leap is web-only (no iOS/Android app)
- JobEscape directly targets career-changers wanting to escape their jobs; Skill Leap targets existing entrepreneurs/professionals
- JobEscape includes automation tool access (N8N, Make); Skill Leap is course-only, no bundled tools
- JobEscape's WhatsApp automation is a unique feature Skill Leap cannot replicate
- For someone starting from zero income, JobEscape's direct freelancing focus is more actionable

## Key Weaknesses / User Complaints

- **No native mobile app**: A significant gap in a mobile-first consumption world
- **No bundled tool access**: Users still need separate subscriptions to the AI tools they're learning about
- **3,000 members is small**: vs. Coursiv's 800K+ paid users — much smaller scale and social proof
- **Course quality varies**: Some users note inconsistency across the 30+ courses
- **Niche audience**: "Entrepreneurs" is a narrower segment than "career changers" or "beginners"
- **Not hands-on enough**: Primarily video-based learning without integrated practice environment
- **Limited freelance/income pathway**: Not explicitly focused on "how to make money" as an outcome

## Notes

- Skill Leap is the most honest/transparent operator in this competitive set — clean pricing, clean reputation
- The Futurepedia connection gives it SEO and authority advantages other small players don't have
- The $29.99/month price point puts it in direct competition with Coursiv and JobEscape
- Lack of a native mobile app is the biggest product gap to exploit
- Would be a strong acquisition target for a larger edtech company given clean brand and Futurepedia alignment
- The 5,500 prompt templates is a moat that would be time-consuming for competitors to replicate
